SEMANTIC POETIC ARCHIVE

Think of this less like reading a document and more like traversing a semantic field.

You don’t need to read top to bottom.

Start anywhere.

The glowing or underlined words are motifs.
Those are the primary interaction anchors.

When you click a motif:

  • the field reorganizes around that concept
  • related semantic structures become active
  • the environment subtly responds
  • connected occurrences emerge across the archive

The breadcrumb trail remembers your traversal path.
It shows the semantic route you’ve taken through the archive.

The cognition map is not a menu.
It’s a relationship field.

Traversal modes change how the system navigates semantic relationships.

You are not expected to fully understand the system immediately.

The interface is designed for gradual discovery.

The best way to explore it is:

  • click a motif
  • observe what changes
  • follow the semantic trail
  • move between related concepts
  • let the archive reveal its own structure over time

There is no single correct reading path.

Research Reflection

This artifact now treats poetry as one semantic source within a broader cognition archive model. Its graph is provisional, probabilistic, and deliberately open to future methods: embeddings, richer metadata, multi-user discourse, and AI-assisted inference.

  • Semantic Recurrence: motifs are extracted through seeded anchors, frequency, density, and contextual co-occurrence.
  • Associative Traversal: ignition activates a state, propagates through weighted relationships, and leaves memory traces behind.
  • Research Infrastructure: dataset loading, relationship scoring, traversal modes, and visual systems are separated so future archives can enter without changing the interface philosophy.

The archive is not a document browser. It is an early semantic engine for moving through thought structures.
